Extra Rules


What Happens When I Level Up?

As you adventure, your character earns experience and eventually levels up. Each new level makes you stronger in a few ways at once. You don't need to memorize the exact numbers, just know what to expect when it happens:

  • More hit points: Your maximum health goes up every single level (see the two methods below).
  • New class features: Your class grants fresh abilities as you climb in level, and this is where a lot of your character's growth comes from.
  • A rising proficiency bonus: At certain levels your proficiency bonus increases (the table back in Step 7), making everything you're trained in better at once.
  • Occasional Ability Score Improvements: At a few milestone levels you get to raise your ability scores, sharpening what your character is best at.

Gaining Hit Points at a Level Up

Every time you level up you add hit points, and there are two ways to do it. Your DM may prefer one or let you choose, so check with them first.

Whichever method you use, always add your Constitution modifier to the hit points you gain each level.

Take the Fixed Average

The simplest option: each class has a set average number tied to its hit die, and you just take that number every level. It's steady and predictable, your health goes up by the same reliable amount each time, with no dice involved.

Roll Your Hit Die

The riskier, more exciting option: roll your class's hit die (for example, a d10 for a Fighter) and gain that many hit points. You might roll high and leap ahead, or roll low and gain very little, it's a gamble.
